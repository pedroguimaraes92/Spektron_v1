# Spektron Scoring Model

## Overview

Spektron introduces an attack-path-centric scoring model that separates **difficulty** from **impact**, avoiding distortion caused by isolated severity metrics.

---

## PathDifficulty (0–10)

PathDifficulty represents the operational effort required to execute the full attack chain.

Factors considered:

* Number of chained steps
* Authentication barriers
* User interaction requirements
* Reliability of techniques
* Automation feasibility

Low scores indicate trivial, highly reliable paths. High scores indicate complex, fragile, or constrained paths.

---

## PathImpact (0–10)

PathImpact represents the severity of the final outcome achievable through the attack path.

Factors considered:

* Data exposure
* Privilege escalation
* System compromise
* Business impact

PathImpact is independent of execution difficulty.

---

## Relationship with CVSS and VPR

CVSS and VPR are treated as **input references**, not decision drivers.

Spektron prioritizes remediation based on:

* High impact, low difficulty paths
* Medium impact paths enabled by weak controls
* Accumulated risk through multiple medium findings

---

## Priority Classification

Final priority is derived from:

PathPriority = f(PathDifficulty, PathImpact, Confidence)

This enables actionable prioritization without overestimating speculative risk.
